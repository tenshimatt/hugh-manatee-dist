# GoHunta Help Documentation & Technical Copy

## Help Center Structure

### Getting Started Section

#### New to GoHunta?
**Quick Start Guide**

**Step 1: Set Up Your Profile**
Your hunting profile helps GoHunta provide personalized recommendations and connect you with relevant community content.

1. Click "Profile" in the main navigation
2. Complete the hunting experience questionnaire
3. Add your primary game species and hunting region
4. Set your privacy preferences for sharing hunt data

**Why this matters:** A complete profile helps our algorithm show you content that matches your experience level and interests.

**Step 2: Log Your First Hunt**
Even if you haven't hunted recently, you can log past hunts to get familiar with the system.

1. Click "Hunt Log" or use the "Quick Log" button
2. Enter basic information: date, location, species, result
3. Add as much detail as you remember - weather, conditions, dog performance
4. Save your entry

**Pro tip:** The more detailed your early logs, the better patterns you'll see as you add more hunts.

**Step 3: Explore the Community**
Connect with hunters who share your interests and experience level.

1. Visit the "Community" section
2. Browse topics that interest you
3. Read community guidelines to understand our culture
4. Consider introducing yourself in the "New Members" forum

**Remember:** Our community values respectful discussion and knowledge sharing. Don't hesitate to ask questions - everyone was new once.

#### Mobile App Basics
**Getting the Most from GoHunta Mobile**

**Offline Functionality**
GoHunta works even without cell service, perfect for remote hunting locations.

**What works offline:**
- Hunt logging with GPS coordinates
- Dog training session recording
- Route navigation (with downloaded maps)
- Photo capture with location tagging
- Voice notes and observations

**What requires connection:**
- Community features and discussions
- Weather data updates
- Map downloads (must download before heading out)
- Photo uploads to community features

**Sync Process:**
When you return to cell service, GoHunta automatically syncs your offline data. You'll see a sync indicator in the top bar showing upload progress.

**Battery Optimization:**
- Enable low-power GPS mode for extended battery life
- Download maps before your hunt to reduce data usage
- Use airplane mode with GPS enabled in remote areas

### Core Features Help

#### Hunt Logging
**Understanding Hunt Log Fields**

**Required Fields:**
- **Date:** When the hunt occurred
- **Location:** General area (be mindful of sharing specific spots publicly)  
- **Species:** Primary species hunted
- **Result:** Outcome of the hunt

**Optional but Valuable Fields:**
- **Weather Conditions:** Temperature, wind, precipitation, cloud cover
- **Hunt Duration:** Start and end times
- **Dogs Used:** Which dogs participated and their performance
- **Equipment:** Gear used during the hunt
- **Notes:** Detailed observations and lessons learned

**Privacy Settings for Hunt Logs:**
- **Private:** Only you can see the details
- **Community:** Other GoHunta users can see general information
- **Public:** Information can appear in community discussions (personal locations remain private)

**Tip:** Even "unsuccessful" hunts provide valuable data. Weather patterns, animal behavior observations, and equipment performance all contribute to better future hunting decisions.

#### Pack Management
**Setting Up Dog Profiles**

**Basic Information:**
- **Name:** Your dog's name
- **Breed:** Specific breed or mix
- **Age/Birth Date:** For tracking development stages
- **Training Level:** Current skill level assessment
- **Health Status:** Overall health and any conditions affecting hunting

**Training Tracking:**
- **Session Type:** Obedience, scent work, retrieval, field work, etc.
- **Duration:** Length of training session
- **Performance Rating:** 1-5 scale for different skills
- **Notes:** Specific observations and areas for improvement

**Performance Analysis:**
GoHunta tracks your dog's performance across different conditions:
- Weather performance (hot, cold, wet conditions)
- Terrain effectiveness (field, marsh, upland, etc.)
- Game species success rates
- Seasonal performance variations

**Health Records:**
Keep vaccination schedules, vet visit notes, and health observations in one place. Set reminders for annual checkups and vaccination renewals.

#### Route Planning
**Creating and Managing Hunt Routes**

**Route Types:**
- **Scouting Routes:** For pre-season area exploration
- **Active Hunt Routes:** Your planned hunting path
- **Training Routes:** Areas for dog training sessions
- **Property Boundaries:** Mark property lines and access points

**Adding Waypoints:**
- **Stand Locations:** Mark tree stands, blinds, or hunting positions
- **Water Sources:** Natural and artificial water features
- **Game Trails:** Observed animal movement patterns
- **Equipment Caches:** Where you store gear or trail cameras
- **Access Points:** Entry and exit locations

**Offline Map Downloads:**
1. Select your hunting area while connected to internet
2. Choose map detail level (higher detail = more storage)
3. Download covers approximately 20-mile radius
4. Maps remain available for 30 days offline

**Sharing Routes:**
Share routes with hunting partners while respecting landowner privacy:
- Remove sensitive waypoints before sharing
- Include access permission details
- Add safety information and emergency contacts

#### Gear Management
**Equipment Tracking System**

**Categories:**
- **Firearms & Ammunition:** Guns, shells, cleaning supplies
- **Dog Equipment:** Collars, leads, training tools, first aid
- **Navigation:** GPS units, compasses, maps, communication devices
- **Clothing:** Weather-appropriate gear, safety equipment
- **Field Processing:** Knives, game bags, coolers
- **Safety Equipment:** First aid, emergency supplies, signaling devices

**Maintenance Tracking:**
- **Service Dates:** When equipment was last serviced or cleaned
- **Condition Assessment:** Current state of each item

- **Performance Notes:** How equipment performed in different conditions

**Loadout Planning:**
Create hunt-specific equipment lists:
- **Early Season Upland:** Light clothing, basic first aid, training gear
- **Late Season Waterfowl:** Cold weather gear, decoys, calls
- **Multi-Day Trip:** Extended supplies, camping gear, extra equipment

**Tips for Success:**
- Update condition status after each hunt
- Set maintenance reminders for critical equipment
- Include backup options for essential items
- Consider weight and space constraints for your hunting style

### Community Features Help

#### BragBoard Guidelines
**Sharing Your Hunting Success**

**Photo Guidelines:**
- Show respectful presentation of harvested game
- Include context that helps others learn (location type, weather, technique)
- Avoid overly graphic images that could damage hunting's public image
- Credit others who contributed to your success

**Story Elements to Include:**
- Preparation and planning details
- Challenges overcome during the hunt
- Lessons learned or techniques used
- Recognition of conservation principles demonstrated
- Acknowledgment of hunting partners, guides, or landowners

**Community Interaction:**
- "Respect" other hunters' achievements (our version of "like")
- Leave constructive comments that add value
- Ask questions that help you learn from their experience
- Share similar experiences when relevant

**What Not to Post:**
- Specific location details that could damage hunting access
- Disrespectful presentation of game animals
- Content that violates fair chase principles
- Purely commercial promotional material

### Troubleshooting Section

#### Common Issues and Solutions

**App Performance Issues**

**Slow Loading or Freezing:**
1. Close and restart the GoHunta app
2. Check your internet connection strength
3. Clear app cache (Android) or force-close app (iOS)
4. Ensure you have the latest app version installed
5. Restart your device if problems persist

**Data Not Syncing:**
1. Check internet connectivity
2. Look for sync indicator in app header
3. Manual sync: Pull down on main screen to refresh
4. Verify account login status
5. Contact support if sync has been failing for more than 4 hours

**GPS/Location Issues:**
1. Enable location services for GoHunta in device settings
2. Choose "Always" or "While Using App" location permission
3. Enable high accuracy GPS mode in device settings
4. Clear GPS cache by toggling airplane mode briefly
5. Download offline maps before hunting in areas with poor cell service

**Battery Drain:**
1. Enable battery optimization for GoHunta in device settings
2. Use low-power GPS mode for extended hunts
3. Download maps and content before going offline
4. Close unnecessary background apps
5. Consider bringing portable charging device for extended trips

#### Account and Data Issues

**Forgot Password:**
1. Use "Forgot Password" link on login screen
2. Check email (including spam folder) for reset instructions
3. Follow link in email to create new password
4. If no email arrives within 10 minutes, check that email address is correct
5. Contact support if reset email never arrives

**Lost Hunt Data:**
1. Check if you're logged into the correct account
2. Verify data sync by checking other devices
3. Data may still be stored locally - try manual sync
4. Check if data was accidentally marked as private
5. Contact support immediately - we can often recover recent data

**Privacy Concerns:**
1. Review privacy settings in account preferences
2. Understand difference between private, community, and public sharing
3. Location data is never shared at GPS-coordinate level
4. Community posts can be edited or deleted at any time
5. Account deletion removes all personal data within 30 days
