### 1. Price Drew's five concerns explicitly into the Monday pitch.

Create a one-page "Answers to Drew's five questions, in writing" handout. His five concerns are the exact frame for answering what Archer hasn't. Hand Drew the page at the meeting; make him the internal advocate.

Scope cost: ~40 engineering hours against ERPNext's native data model — effectively free at our velocity.

### 3. Pre-commit to an Epicor cutover scenario on the quote itself — don't leave it open.

Both Drew and Collin asked "is Epicor being replaced?" The uncertainty itself is the objection. Quote Scenario C (parallel run, 3–6 months, then decommission) as the default, with Scenario A (clean cut) as a $5K discount alternative. Taking the ambiguity off the table flips the conversation from "Archer doesn't know" to "sovereign.ai has a plan."

### 4. Lead with the timeline delta as the commercial headline.

Archer: discovery complete Apr 2026 → go-live **Jan 2027** = 8 months of build + test.
sovereign.ai: demo running NOW → go-live **pre-Australia Jul/Aug 2026** = 3 months.

**Chris gets 5 months of operational benefit earlier.** At $30M → $100M trajectory, 5 months of OTD and scrap visibility is worth more than the entire $55K fee. Lead with this math, not the tech stack.

### 5. Neutralize the "Archer gate" by offering a parallel/shadow build at zero risk.
